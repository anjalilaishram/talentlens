# Senior AI Engineer — Founding Team

**Company:** Redrob AI (Series A AI-native talent intelligence platform)
**Location:** Pune / Noida, India (Hybrid, flexible) — open to relocation from Tier-1 Indian cities
**Experience:** 5–9 years (a guide, not a hard gate)

## The mandate

Own the intelligence layer of the product: the ranking, retrieval and matching
systems that decide what recruiters see when they search for candidates, and what
candidates see when they search for roles. The current stack is mostly BM25 +
rule-based scoring — working, but not great. You will ship a v2 that demonstrably
improves recruiter-engagement metrics (embeddings, hybrid retrieval, LLM-based
re-ranking), then build the evaluation infrastructure (offline benchmarks, online
A/B testing, recruiter-feedback loops) so the team keeps improving without flying
blind. You will also drive the long-term architecture of candidate–JD matching at
scale and mentor new hires.

We want deep technical depth in modern ML systems **and** a scrappy
product-engineering attitude — someone who will ship a working ranker in a week even
when the ML is "obviously suboptimal", because we learn from real users first.

## Things you absolutely need

- **Embeddings-based retrieval in production** (sentence-transformers, OpenAI
  embeddings, BGE, E5 or similar) deployed to real users — you have handled embedding
  drift, index refresh, retrieval-quality regression.
- **Vector databases / hybrid search in production** — Pinecone, Weaviate, Qdrant,
  Milvus, OpenSearch, Elasticsearch, FAISS, pgvector or similar. The operational
  experience matters more than the specific tech.
- **Strong Python.** Code quality matters.
- **Evaluation of ranking systems** — NDCG, MRR, MAP, offline-to-online correlation,
  A/B test interpretation.

## Nice to have

- LLM fine-tuning (LoRA, QLoRA, PEFT)
- Learning-to-rank models (XGBoost-based or neural)
- HR-tech / recruiting-tech / marketplace products
- Distributed systems or large-scale inference optimization
- Open-source contributions in AI/ML

## Explicitly NOT a fit

- **Title-chasers** who switch companies every ~1.5 years for the next title.
- **Framework enthusiasts** whose work is mostly LangChain tutorials / demos.
- **Pure consulting-services careers** (TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Accenture, Cognizant,
  Capgemini, etc.) with no product-company experience.
- **Computer-vision / speech / robotics** specialists without significant NLP/IR.
- **Pure research** (academic / research-only) with no production deployment.
- **Recent LangChain-on-OpenAI only** (<12 months) with no pre-LLM-era ML production.

## How to read between the lines

The right answer is not "most AI keywords". A candidate whose career history shows
they built a recommendation system at a product company is a fit even if they never
wrote "RAG" or "Pinecone". A candidate with every AI keyword in their skills list
but whose title is "Marketing Manager" is **not** a fit. Weigh behavioural signals
too: a perfect-on-paper candidate who has not logged in for six months with a 5%
recruiter-response rate is, for hiring purposes, not actually available.

We expect few matches in a 100K pool, and we are fine with that — better to see 10
great matches than 1000 maybes.
